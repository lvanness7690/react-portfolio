Zincstream and InviteMeNow Platform

Streaming, registration, and client-facing event experience systems

Overview

At Zinc Agency, I helped build Zincstream, a proprietary streaming and registration platform created during the COVID pivot to keep premium client programming running. When live events returned, I turned that same infrastructure into InviteMeNow, a reusable registration and event-information platform for in-person, virtual, and hybrid hospitality experiences.

Business Context

The team needed more than a generic webinar or event-registration tool. We were delivering branded, high-touch experiences for corporate clients, often centered around celebrity talent, hospitality programming, and white-glove guest communication. Off-the-shelf tools made it harder to control the experience and often forced the team into expensive SaaS subscriptions that did not fit the workflow cleanly.

Problem

There were two phases to solve. During COVID, the business needed a way to keep programming alive through virtual events without losing brand control or client confidence. After in-person events returned, the team needed to reuse that investment as a durable registration and event-delivery system instead of discarding it and going back to fragmented third-party tools.

What I Built

  • Built custom streaming and registration workflows using WordPress, JavaScript, HTML, CSS, and embedded meeting and video APIs including Whereby
  • Helped support more than 2,000 celebrity-led livestreams during the virtual programming pivot
  • Managed technical specifications and coordinated execution across clients, talent, crews, production partners, and internal stakeholders
  • Repurposed the virtual-event infrastructure into InviteMeNow, a reusable platform for in-person, virtual, and hybrid event registration
  • Built branded event landing pages with reusable content modules such as registration states, thank-you states, speaker or chef bios, venue details, maps and directions, image galleries, support forms, and event-specific information blocks
  • Supported custom URLs and subdomains so programs could feel client-owned rather than generic
  • Helped create a practical in-house alternative to event SaaS subscriptions such as Splash for recurring hospitality programs

What The Live Product Shows

The current InviteMeNow demo makes the platform value more concrete. A single event page can handle branded registration and post-registration thank-you logic, calendar-add flows, talent or speaker biography sections, venue and location details, photo-gallery modules, embedded maps and directions, and support or registration-help forms.

That matters because the platform is not just a landing page builder. It is an operational system for presenting event information, handling guest registration states, and supporting the client-facing experience around an event.

Tools and Systems

WordPress, JavaScript, HTML, CSS, Whereby and embedded meeting/video APIs, registration workflows and front-end state logic, branded event pages, custom URLs and subdomains, and event content modules covering galleries, maps, and support flows.

Impact

This project shows that I can build and evolve a business-critical system under changing operating conditions. It started as a virtual-event survival tool, then became a reusable platform for client delivery. That combination of technical implementation, workflow design, and operational reuse translates well to implementation, customer success, technical account, product operations, and internal systems roles.
